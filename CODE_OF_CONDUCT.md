# Code of Conduct

## 1. Purpose

The Music Client project is an open-source community built on trust, collaboration, and respect. This Code of Conduct exists to set clear expectations for all participants — contributors, maintainers, users, reviewers, and anyone else interacting within our community spaces — ensuring a consistent, professional, and harassment-free environment for everyone.

We believe that a diverse and inclusive community produces better, more innovative software. This document is not a list of punishments, but a statement of shared values.

---

## 2. Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- All GitHub repositories under this project
- Issue trackers, pull request threads, and commit messages
- Project discussion boards, wikis, and documentation
- Official project communication channels (chat, forums, email lists)
- Any in-person or online event where a participant represents the project

It applies to **all participants** regardless of their level of experience, background, or role within the project.

---

## 3. Our Standards

### 3.1 Expected Behaviour

All participants are expected to:

- **Be respectful and considerate.** Treat all community members with professionalism and courtesy. Disagreements are natural; hostility is not.
- **Use welcoming and inclusive language.** Avoid slang, jargon, or phrasing that may alienate newcomers or non-native speakers.
- **Give and receive constructive feedback graciously.** Frame technical criticism around the code, not the person. Accept feedback on your own contributions with openness.
- **Acknowledge and credit others' work.** Attribute ideas, code, or approaches to their source. Do not claim credit for contributions that are not yours.
- **Prioritize the health of the project.** When personal preferences conflict with the broader community's needs, seek compromise through discussion.
- **Be patient.** Open source contributors operate across time zones with varying availability. Allow reasonable time for responses.

### 3.2 Unacceptable Behaviour

The following behaviours will not be tolerated under any circumstances:

- **Harassment of any kind**, including but not limited to: unwelcome personal remarks, sexual attention, deliberate intimidation, or threats.
- **Discriminatory language or conduct** targeting any individual or group on the basis of gender, gender identity or expression, race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, age, disability, sexual orientation, or any other characteristic.
- **Public or private disparagement** of contributors or their work beyond reasonable, evidence-based technical critique.
- **Trolling**, deliberately inflammatory or off-topic comments, or bad-faith engagement.
- **Doxxing** — publishing private personal information (physical address, email, employer, etc.) without explicit consent.
- **Spamming or self-promotion** unrelated to the project in community channels.
- **Knowingly submitting plagiarised or AI-generated code** that has not been adequately reviewed and disclosed (see `CONTRIBUTING.md`).

---

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

### 4.1 Contributors
Any individual submitting code, documentation, or feedback is expected to have read and agreed to this Code of Conduct before participating.

### 4.2 Maintainers
Project maintainers are responsible for:
- Clarifying standards of acceptable behaviour when ambiguity arises.
- Reviewing reported incidents fairly, promptly, and without bias.
- Taking corrective action proportional to the severity of the violation.
- Preserving the confidentiality of reporters to the extent possible.

Maintainers have the authority to moderate content (edit, hide, or delete comments) and to temporarily or permanently suspend participants whose behaviour is in violation of this Code of Conduct.

---

## 5. Reporting an Incident

If you witness or experience behaviour that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it. Reports are treated seriously and handled confidentially.

**To report an incident:**
1. Email the project maintainers at the address listed in the repository's contact information.
2. Include a clear description of the incident, relevant links or screenshots, and the names of individuals involved.
3. Indicate whether you are the direct target of the behaviour or a bystander witness.

You will receive an acknowledgement within **72 hours** and a resolution or status update within **7 calendar days**.

---

## 6. Enforcement

Violations will be evaluated on a case-by-case basis. Possible responses include:

| Severity | Example | Response |
|---|---|---|
| **Minor** | Unintentionally dismissive comment | Private correction and guidance |
| **Moderate** | Repeated disrespectful behaviour | Formal warning, temporary comment restriction |
| **Serious** | Targeted harassment or doxxing | Temporary or permanent ban from all project spaces |
| **Critical** | Threats, illegal activity | Immediate permanent ban and, where appropriate, escalation to legal authorities |

All enforcement decisions will be made without regard to the contributor's standing, prior contributions, or relationships within the project.

---

## 7. Appeals

A participant who believes they have been subject to an unfair enforcement action may submit an appeal by contacting the maintainer team with a written explanation. Appeals will be reviewed by a different maintainer than the one who issued the original decision, where possible.

---

## 8. Amendments

This Code of Conduct may be updated periodically to reflect the evolving needs of the community. Significant changes will be announced via the project's main communication channels. Continued participation following a revision constitutes acceptance of the updated terms.
